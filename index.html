How I work

The cost of producing UI has collapsed. What used to take a week of Figma now takes an afternoon in Cursor with Claude Code. That changes where the value sits — less in production, more in deciding what's worth producing in the first place. So that's where I spend my time: framing the problem, choosing the direction, then building the prototype myself rather than describing it.

In practice that looks like this: I start with the outcome and the constraints, sketch the shape of the solution, then move into a coded prototype early — usually Vue or React inside an existing design system, deployed to a branch URL that stakeholders can actually use. That's how the day-to-day work at Defrr and Galvanized Pukeko gets shipped. The property-platform design challenge above — buyer and seller side — was built the same way, end to end.

I've spent the last year deliberately closing the technical gap that used to sit between designers and engineers. IBM's AI Product Manager Professional Certificate and four Generative AI courses through IBM SkillsBuild covered the model and product fundamentals; Google Cloud's Conversational AI on Vertex AI and Dialogflow CX gave me hands-on experience designing for agent behaviour; LinkedIn Learning's AI Product Ideation track filled in the discovery side. Combined with shipping production code at Defrr and Galvanized Pukeko, it means I can discuss trade-offs with engineers as a peer, not hand off a static file and wait.

The constant across all of it is taste. AI gives you a starting point. Whether it ships as something coherent or something generic — that's still a human call.

Working with me

“The board says we need AI in the product. By Q3. Figure it out.”

That brief is landing on product teams everywhere — and most don't have anyone who has actually built with a model, wired up an agent, or shipped a tool call to production. What they need isn't another vendor deck or another chat box bolted onto search. It's someone who can hold the trade-offs under sceptical questioning, and who understands both the AI architecture and the product surface.

The property platform above is the worked example. Not a chat box pasted on the side — a product redesigned around what AI is actually good at: the suggests, never decides posture, the router-and-specialist architecture, sourced data only, and the agent-economics trade-off named honestly rather than hidden.

If your board has handed you a brief that looks like that — this is the kind of conversation I have for a living.
