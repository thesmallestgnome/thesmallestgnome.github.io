OneRoof Guide
Concept Summary · Design Challenge
Mari Kondrateva · April 2026
A mobile-first agentic chat for property discovery
Built as a functional HTML prototype rather than a Figma mock — the workflow described in "Operating as an AI-native product designer in 2026". A Smart Search variant (AI populates the filter, familiar list view) was considered as a lower-risk stepping stone; the recommendation below is Agentic Chat.

The Problem

OneRoof has 20+ filters, suburb profiles, estimates, school zones, commute times and council data — each in a separate tool. Users translate real questions ("near a good school, 30 min to work, not on a busy road") into checkboxes, then cross-reference by hand. App Store reviews confirm the gap: keyword search requests ("do-up", "urgent sale"), "For You" that can't be filtered, saved searches that forget what matters. The data is rich; the intelligence connecting it isn't.

The Opportunity

No major NZ portal offers AI search. Trade Me is generalist, filter-only. Realestate.co.nz has strong maps, no AI. Homes.co.nz is a data reference tool. Internationally, Zillow launched NL search in 2023 and full conversational AI Mode in Mar 2026; Homes.com and Realtor.com followed. NZ is wide open and OneRoof has the strongest data to move first.

Who We're Designing For

First-home buyers (anxious, under-educated, highest stakes), move-up buyers (time-pressed, juggling sell+buy), investors (yield-focused, fast to shortlist). Secondary: agents (paying customers — they want qualified leads, not volume), plus sellers, renters and property-curious owners.

The Concept — Agentic Chat

A conversational Guide drives the full journey: user types intent in their own words; the Router streams working steps with data-source pills (MoE, Maps, OneRoof) while the Search Specialist works in parallel; property cards arrive inline; multi-turn follow-ups ("walk time to a primary?") re-rank the shortlist in place without re-searching. 6 screens — onboarding, three chat turns (search, follow-up, compare), property detail with Guide Insights, and a For You nudge surface. Filter search stays one tap away as the escape hatch.

Why straight to Agentic Chat. 79% of NZ adults already use conversational AI — chat isn't unfamiliar, it's the pattern users arrive pre-trained on. A Smart Search variant (NL populates filter; ranked list) was considered as a lower-risk stepping stone; shipping it spends budget on a UX the market has moved past. Put the saved effort into agents, evals and data quality.

Design Principles

User stays in controlSuggests, never decides. Filter search always available.
Transparency over magicEvery recommendation shows reasoning. No black boxes.
Earned autonomyStarts conservative; becomes proactive with opt-in.
Mobile-first & glanceableProperty cards carry the weight; conversation stays tight and purposeful.
Augment, don't replaceOpen homes and agents stay central. Guide prepares users.
Trust is the productCommercial interests never compromise neutrality.

Technical Architecture — Multi-Agent System

User input → Router (Haiku) → Specialist (Sonnet)

Router (lightweight, cheap, fast) classifies intent and streams transparency messages on-screen ("Understanding your needs…", "Searching 46,000+ listings…", "Checking school zones · MoE") while the specialist works in parallel. Messages build trust and mask latency.

Specialists (Search · Insights · Comparison · Coach) each handle one capability — swappable, individually observable, invoked only when justified. Conversation state lets follow-ups re-rank without re-searching.

What This Means for OneRoof's Design System

Reuses existing atoms — cards, type, tab bar, spacing. The net-new organism is the chat surface: message bubbles, streamed working-step list with data-source pills, inline property cards, turn divider, Stop control, and a Guide Insights card on the detail page. With the OneRoof codebase and Figma library in hand, Claude Design (Anthropic, launched 17 April) would generate this against the real tokens — shortening concept-to-prototype from weeks to days.

Business Model & Commercials

Free for end users. Search is top-of-funnel; paywalling it hands differentiation to Trade Me. Monetisation flows through existing streams, amplified: better-qualified enquiries justify premium agent listings and subscriptions. Cost containment: Router-first + caching of suburb/school/commute data keeps per-query cost in single-digit NZ cents. Future revenue: agent-facing AI tools, labelled sponsored new-builds, home-loan partner integration. Avoided: charging users, hiding listings behind AI, compromising neutrality.

Key Metrics & KPIs

Engagement Time-to-shortlist (target −30%); weekly active on "For You"; search-to-enquiry conversion; return sessions per saved property
Lead Quality Enquiry-to-viewing rate (agent-reported); agent NPS on leads (+15pts goal); impression-to-enquiry ratio vs. filter search
Trust & Health Suggestion acceptance (40–70%); reversion to filter search (<15%); explainability click-through; negative feedback (<5%)

Testing & Validation

Pre-launch eval — ~200 real NZ queries (saved-search logs + App Store review language) with expected top-5, scored by property experts. Every agent change passes the eval before shipping. Moderated usability — 6–8 users per segment (first-home, move-up, investor), recruited from existing saved-search users. Can they articulate what they want? Do they trust the reasoning? Actively analyse feedback. Iterate.
